SOA Exam Fee Proposal – Briefing Memo for July 2026 Board Meeting
Prepared by: CEO's Office / Education Finance
Date: July 2026

DECISION REQUESTED
Approve a 4% exam-fee adjustment effective January 2027 (spring sitting registration cycle).

CONTEXT
- Vendor proctoring costs have risen 12% since 2024. Current fees no longer fully cover delivery cost.
- Peer benchmark: SOA exam fees sit 8–15% below comparable credentialing bodies.
- Incremental revenue funds the digital study platform members requested in the 2025 member survey.

FINANCIAL PATHS (2027 plan impact)
1. Hold fees flat
   - No candidate friction
   - -$1.2M vs plan in 2027
   - Forces a revisit under cost pressure in Q4 2026

2. Raise 4% (recommended)
   - Covers proctoring cost growth
   - Still below peer bodies after the adjustment
   - +$0.9M to plan; funds study platform build

MEMBERSHIP & EXAM HEADLINE (Q2 2026)
- Total members: 33,412 (+2.1% vs Q1) – 6th consecutive quarter of growth (longest streak since 2019)
- New FSAs YTD: 1,204 (+9% vs 2025 pace); FY target 2,600
- Lapse rate: 4.3% (-0.4 pts); FY target 4.5%
- FY membership target: 34,000
- Preliminary exam sittings dipped in Q2 to 7,410 (from 7,980 in Q1); fellowship sittings rose to 2,300
- Dip concentrated in Exam P (-9.5%) and Exam FM (-9.8%); FAM and ALTAM nearly flat
- Candidate pipeline: 66% university programs, 24% employer-sponsored, 10% independent
  → University calendar shift is the leading explanation for the prelim dip; fellowship demand is stable

ROLLOUT (if approved July 2026)
- Jul 2026: Board approval
- Sep 2026: Member communication
- Nov 2026: Registration opens at new fees
- Jan 2027: New fees effective

EXECUTION READINESS (as of July 2026)
- Fee model & pricing: 90% complete
- Member comms plan: 65% complete
- Registration systems: 40% complete
All workstreams on schedule for the January effective date.

RECOMMENDATION
Approve the 4% exam-fee adjustment effective January 2027.
