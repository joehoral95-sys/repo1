# Brief: july-board-exam-fees

- **Audience:** SOA Board of Directors – strategic, time-poor, numbers-fluent.
  They want the decision framed, not the raw data dump.
- **Intent:** Get sign-off on the 4% exam-fee proposal effective January 2027.
  Secondary: reassure that membership is healthy and the prelim dip is a
  pipeline issue, not a retention problem.
- **Date / occasion:** July 2026 board meeting, short decision slot.
- **Sources:** `inbox/` briefing pack (membership/exam volumes, exam detail,
  pipeline mix, fee-proposal memo) → digests in `sources/`.
- **Candidate insights (verified):**
  1. **Membership grew for a 6th straight quarter** to 33,412 (+2.1% vs Q1) –
     longest streak since 2019. Lapse 4.3% is under the 4.5% FY target.
     Lead with strength before the ask.
  2. **The exam dip is concentrated in prelims; fellowship is rising** –
     prelims 7,980 → 7,410 in Q2; fellowship 2,260 → 2,300. Pipeline issue,
     not retention. Reframes the bad news.
  3. **P and FM carry the dip** – together ~62–64% of prelim volume, each
     down ~10%; FAM and ALTAM nearly flat. Concentration finding from
     `insights.md`, verified against exam-detail digest.
  4. **Two-thirds of new candidates arrive via universities** (66%) – makes
     the university-calendar explanation for the prelim dip checkable and
     actionable.
  5. **Holding fees flat costs $1.2M vs plan; 4% still leaves SOA below peers**
     (8–15% below comparable bodies) while covering a 12% proctoring-cost
     rise and adding +$0.9M to plan. Kills the main objection.
- **Ask (verbatim):** Approve the 4% exam-fee adjustment effective January 2027.
